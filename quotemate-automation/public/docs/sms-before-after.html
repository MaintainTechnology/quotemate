How a quote is made — old vs new

Point by point

Before & after, side by side

◀ BeforeAfter ▶
01

Who sets the price

Before

The AI wrote the dollar figures on every quote.

After

A fixed recipe in the database sets the price. The AI only reads the job and picks the recipe.

02

Same job, same price?

Before

No. The same job could come out at a different total each time it was quoted.

After

Yes. The same job gives the same total, every time.

03

Are the prices real?

Before

Estimates only. Never checked against real supplier or trade rates, so accuracy was unknown.

After

Calibrated to real Australian prices, and measured against tradie-checked examples before any job goes live.

04

Materials catalogue

Before

Gaps — a wrong category on the most common job, blank brands, and duplicate products.

After

Cleaned and guarded. The top job is fixed; remaining gaps are flagged for real data, never made up.

05

Safety checks

Before

Several checks ran in "watch only" mode, so a wrong product could still be sent.

After

Checks now enforce. A wrong product, or a total that is too high or low, is held back instead of sent.

06

Sending quotes

Before

Every clean quote auto-sent to the customer, with no gate.

After

Auto-send is off by default and earned one job type at a time. A single switch turns it all off.

07

Getting the right job

Before

The job type was guessed in three places, with no check if they disagreed — so it could price the wrong job.

After

The three are cross-checked. On a disagreement it asks one question, or books an inspection.

08

The conversation

Before

Could drop a good lead over a missing name, or loop a slow-but-willing customer to inspection.

After

Only details that affect the price can block a quote. A cooperative customer is never cut off.

09

Visibility

Before

No record of how a quote was priced or whether it was accurate.

After

Every quote records how it was priced. A dashboard shows the mix and confirms zero unchecked sends.

Before go-live

Four steps left to switch it on

01

Run one database update

Migration 130 — fixes the pricing on the most common job (downlights).

02

Flip two switches

Turn on deterministic pricing and the list of jobs allowed to auto-send.

03

Load real data

Real Australian prices and rates, plus a set of tradie-checked example quotes to measure against.

04

Live test, then roll out

Send a few real test texts, then turn on auto-send one job type at a time.

The first two steps take minutes. The third is the real work — gathering verified prices. The system stays safe (human review or paid inspection) until each step is done.
