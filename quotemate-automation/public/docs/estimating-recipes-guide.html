Recipes & Estimating

The two tabs that decide how your AI prices a job

When a customer texts your number, the AI receptionist works out what the job is — then prices it using the settings on these two tabs. This guide explains what each one does, what you need to set up (mostly nothing), and what happens when you change things.

How a quote is built

The assembly line behind every SMS quote

Every quote follows the same five steps, in the same order. The bold parts are what these two tabs control.

Customer texts your numberThe AI receptionist chats with them and works out which job they need — say, “replace a couple of downlights”.
The recipe supplies the parts listYour recipe if you’ve made one, otherwise the standard one. This is why the same job always gets the same parts.
Your catalogue supplies the pricesEach part is matched to a product in your Catalogue by its category. No match? It falls back to a generic price.
Labour and markup are addedThe job’s labour hours × your hourly rate, plus your markup. Defaults come from your Pricing tab; the Estimating tab can override them per job.
The quote is sent by SMSThe customer gets a link to a mobile quote page. Every price on it traces back to steps 2–4.
Safety net: the AI is never allowed to invent a price. If it can’t ground every line in your pricing data, the job is routed to a paid inspection instead of a guessed quote.
Tab 1 · Recipes

Recipes — the parts list a job always needs

A recipe is the ingredient list for one job. It answers a single question: “when I do this job, what parts do I always use, and how many?”

Every job starts with a sensible standard recipe written by us. You only touch this tab when your way of doing a job differs — you always fit 8 downlights instead of 6, or you always add a smoke-alarm check.

What a recipe looks like

Install security camera (single) — recipe Electrical
Parts in this recipe
  • 1 × security camera — Eufy wireless battery-operated ⚠ No catalogue product — generic price
Each part has a quantity, an optional description, and a “required” tick — required parts are always quoted.

The one rule that matters

Each part in a recipe has a material category (like “downlight” or “security_camera”). The AI uses that category to look up your product in the Catalogue tab. So:

  • Category matches a Catalogue product → the quote uses your real product name, brand and price. ✓ Your catalogue
  • No matching product → the quote falls back to a generic industry price. ⚠ Generic price

If you see the amber “generic price” badge, the fix is not on this tab — add the product to your Catalogue using the same category, and the recipe picks it up automatically.

What happens when you edit a recipe

  • Your version replaces the standard one for you only — from the next quote onward. Other tradies are never affected, and quotes already sent don’t change.
  • Start from our baseline and adjust it — you rarely need to build from scratch.
  • Get a quantity wrong and every future quote for that job inherits the mistake — that’s the power and the risk. The Estimating tab (below) is where you sanity-check it.
Tab 2 · Estimating

Estimating — see how a job prices before a customer does

This tab is a read-out, not a form. For every job you offer, it shows the exact parts, labour hours and markup the AI will use — and where each value comes from. Think of it as running a job through the AI as a drill.

How to read a job card

The badges, decoded

BadgeWhat it means
Standard recipeThis job uses our baseline parts list. Fine for most jobs — edit it on the Recipes tab only if your way differs.
✓ Your catalogueThis part is priced from a real product you added in Catalogue — your brand, your price. This is the goal state.
⚠ Generic priceNo matching Catalogue product, so a generic industry price is used. Works, but it’s not your number — add the product to Catalogue to fix it.
Global defaultLabour hours or markup come from your overall settings on the Pricing tab. One number, all jobs.

The two buttons

  • Edit overrides — set your own labour hours or markup for that one job only. Example: the default says 0.4 hr to replace a downlight, but your crew takes 0.75 hr — override it, and every future quote for that job uses your number.
  • Reset to default — clears your override and the job goes back to the global setting. Nothing is ever lost permanently.
Straight answer

Are these tabs essential?

Recipes

The engine’s backbone

Recipes are why the same job gets the same quote every time. You can leave them on the standard baseline forever — but the standard can’t know that you fit 8 downlights, not 6. Editing them is how the AI learns your way of working.

Impact on quotes: high · Setup required: none
Estimating

Your trust check

Estimating changes nothing by itself — it shows you the maths and lets you fine-tune labour and markup per job. Skipping it won’t break anything, but reviewing your top jobs here is the single best way to trust what the AI sends customers.

Impact on quotes: medium · Setup required: none
Bottom line: the engine works on day one with zero setup — safe defaults cover everything. These tabs exist so the quotes stop being generic and start being yours. Ten minutes here is the difference.
Do this once

The 10-minute setup

  1. 1.Open Estimating and skim your five most common jobs. Do the parts, hours and markup look like how you’d actually quote them?
  2. 2.Hunt the amber generic price badges. For each one, add your real product in Catalogue under the same category — the badge flips to “your catalogue” on its own.
  3. 3.Where the labour hours are wrong for your crew, hit Edit overrides and set your own. Don’t touch the ones that look right.
  4. 4.Only if a parts list is truly different from how you work, adjust the recipe on the Recipes tab. Most tradies never need to.
Common questions

Quick answers

What if I never touch these tabs?

Quotes still go out, priced from the standard recipes, generic prices and your global rates. They’ll be sensible — just not tailored to your products and your pace of work.

Can my changes affect other tradies?

No. Recipes, overrides and catalogue products are yours alone. Editing them changes your quotes only.

Why does a part say “generic price”?

The recipe asks for a category (say, “security_camera”) that has no matching product in your Catalogue. Add one under that exact category and your real price takes over.

I changed something and regret it — how do I undo?

Labour or markup overrides: hit Reset to default on the job’s card in Estimating. Recipe edits: adjust or remove the lines you added on the Recipes tab. Already-sent quotes never change retroactively.
