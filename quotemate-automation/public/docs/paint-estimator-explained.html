Paint estimator

How a paint quote
gets its price.

A plain-English walk through the exact maths behind every Good, Better and Best number, plus a live calculator you can poke at.

In one sentence Measure each surface, multiply it by a rate, adjust for the job, add it up, then split the total into three price tiers.

The one big idea

Every price is surface times a rate.

The estimator does not guess a lump sum. It works the way you would buy carpet or tiles: it figures out how much surface there is, then multiplies by a price per square metre (or per metre of length for trim). Everything else is a small adjustment on top of that.

The units it counts

Four kinds of surface, two kinds of unit:

Walls Ceilings Exterior counted in m² (square metres of area).

Trim (skirting, architraves) counted in lm (linear metres, just length).

So do we pick "square metre" or "hourly"?

You already have one calculator that does both. The default is priced by the square metre. There is an optional "hourly" mode, but it does not add up hours worked. It just turns an hourly rate into an equivalent per square metre rate, then runs the exact same maths. More on this two sections down.

Measure the surfaces

From the address (or a manual entry) it works out one floor-area number, then fans that out into wall m², ceiling m², trim length and exterior m². Each comes with a low and high band, because a guessed area is never exact.

walls m² = floor area × wall_multiplier | ceiling m² = floor area

Set the rate per unit

Each surface has a price per unit from the rate card you tune in the dashboard. Default: walls $28/m², ceilings $20/m², trim $12/lm, exterior $45/m². (In hourly mode this rate is instead worked out as hourly rate divided by how many units you paint per hour.)

rate = rate_card[surface] | hourly mode: rate = hourly_rate ÷ units_per_hour

Adjust for the job

The rate is a starting point for a standard job. It then gets nudged up or down for how many coats, how rough the surface is, and whether the colour is changing.

adjust = coats × condition × colour_change coats: 1→0.70 2→1.00 3→1.35 condition: sound 1.00 minor +15% bare +40% colour change +10%

Add it all up (this is "Better")

For every surface: quantity times rate times the adjustment. Exterior gets an extra loading (+50%) if it is a two-storey job, because access is harder. Add every surface together and you have the middle "Better" price.

Better = Σ ( quantity × rate × adjust × storey_loading )

Split into three tiers

Good and Best are simply the Better price scaled. Good is a lighter refresh, Best is premium prep and paint. No separate calculation, just a multiplier.

Good = Better × 0.72 | Better × 1.00 | Best = Better × 1.28

Apply the floor, then GST

Tiny jobs are lifted to a minimum call-out ($450). Then the 10% GST is added to show the price the customer actually pays. Every tier is also worked out at its low and high band, which is where the price range comes from.

final = max( tier, $450 ) × 1.10

Two ways to set the rate

"Hourly" is just another way to write "per square metre".

This is the heart of your question. The engine never adds up hours to build a total. If a painter prefers to think in dollars per hour, the engine converts that into a per square metre rate and then runs the identical maths. The two roads meet.

By the square metre default

You type a rate straight in. Walls are $28 for every square metre. Simple, and it is what most painters quote in.

By the hour optional

You give an hourly rate ($85/hr) and how fast the crew paints (3 m² per hour on walls). The engine divides: $85 ÷ 3 = $28.33/m². Same shape, different starting number.

Both land in almost the same place (by design)

Per square metre $28.00 / m²
Hourly, converted $28.33 / m²

The defaults were chosen so $85/hr at 3 m²/hr comes out to roughly the $28/m² wall rate. Picking "hourly" does not change how tiers, loadings, the floor or GST behave. Only the base rate is written a different way.

Back to your question

Do we need a "sqm or hourly" selector?

You already have the calculator that handles both.

It is one engine with a per-painter switch. Everything after the base rate (coats, prep, loadings, tiers, floor, GST) is shared. So there is nothing to build from scratch. The only gaps are that the dashboard screen does not yet show the hourly switch, and that "hourly" today is a way to write the rate, not a true add-up-the-hours quote.

Recommendation

Keep "per square metre" as the default. Do not force painters to choose. Offer "hourly" as an optional advanced toggle for those who think in dollars per hour. A required either-or adds friction for a choice that lands in the same place.

The one catch to remember

Hourly still needs the measured area. It divides area by a paint-per-hour speed to get hours. So switching to hourly does not remove the need to measure the surfaces. Measurement is load-bearing either way.
