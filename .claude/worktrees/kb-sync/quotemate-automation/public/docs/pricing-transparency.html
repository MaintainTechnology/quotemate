QuoteMate · Pricing transparency

How QuoteMate prices a job

How a quote is built — from the customer's call or text, through the AI engines, into the price database, and back as a quote. What the AI does, what the database does, and where the line between them sits.

01 · The short answer

Does the AI set the price?

The AI drafts the quote and proposes the numbers. A separate, non-AI check then verifies every price against the tradie's price list.

If a price doesn't match the list, the whole quote is rejected and the customer is offered a paid site visit. A made-up price cannot reach a customer.

02 · The journey

From call to quote — four stages

A quote passes through four stages. Two are run by the AI, one is the conversation, and the last is plain code. The price database sits beside them.

Diagram 2 · The four stages and the price database
Stage 1 · Communication

The customer explains the job

A phone call or text. On SMS an AI agent runs the chat and keeps asking until it has the details a quote needs. Nothing is priced here.

Stage 2 · Intake engine — AI

The conversation becomes structured data

Claude reads the transcript and photos and records the job in a fixed format — type, item count, scope, access, risks. This is the AI's reading of the job; still no prices.

Stage 3 · Estimation engine — AI

Claude drafts the quote and looks up the prices

Claude picks the items, sets quantities and labour hours, and looks up each price from the database. It then writes the full Good / Better / Best draft.

Stage 4 · Grounding check — plain code

Every price is verified against the database

Non-AI code re-checks each price against the database. All match — the quote is sent. Any mismatch — the whole quote becomes a paid site visit. This is what makes the pricing trustworthy.

3

The estimation engine looks up prices and drafts the quote

Estimation engine · AI

Claude calls lookup tools that read straight from the price list and catalogue — it cannot price anything without them:

lookup_assembly("install LED downlight") └─ DATABASE returns "Install LED downlight" · 0.5 hr labour per light lookup_material("warm white LED downlight") └─ DATABASE returns 3 catalogue rows, raw prices: Basic LED downlight .......... $28.00 Tri-colour LED downlight ..... $44.00 Dimmable IP-rated downlight .. $72.00 apply_markup($28.00, markup 28%) └─ returns $35.84 (28% markup — from the tradie's pricing book)

With real prices in hand, it drafts the Good tier:

4

The grounding check verifies every price

Grounding check · plain code

Non-AI code re-checks each line against the database:

  • ✓ $35.84 per downlight — matches catalogue "Basic LED downlight" ($28.00) plus the tradie's 28% markup.
  • ✓ $110.00 / hr labour — matches the pricing book's hourly rate exactly.
  • ✓ 3.0 hr labour total — above the tradie's 2.0 hr minimum-job rule.

All lines verified — the quote is sent. One mismatch would have sent it to a paid site visit instead.

04 · No sugar-coating

What's the AI, what's the database

Every part of a quote, and how much of it is checked against the database.

In short: the rates and prices are locked to the database — the AI cannot send a made-up rate. But the size of the job — item counts and labour hours — is the AI's estimate of what the customer described.

05 · Local overrides

Where the tradie's prices take over

QuoteMate ships a shared default price list so a new tradie isn't blank on day one. Each tradie's own numbers replace it — and the tradie's numbers always win.

06 · The real guarantees

What it does — and doesn't — promise

What it guarantees

The rates are real

  • No price the AI invents can reach a customer
  • Every price on a sent quote is a real price-list figure
  • If the numbers don't line up, the quote is pulled — never patched
  • The customer gets a verified quote or an honest paid site visit
What it does not

The job size is judged

  • Item counts and labour hours are the AI's read of the job
  • The same job described differently can come out differently
  • It is not yet a fixed calculator
  • The price check allows small rounding tolerance

The honest summary: QuoteMate is an AI estimator with a hard database price-check on the end. Its reliability claim is specific and defensible — the prices a customer sees are always real prices from the tradie's own list.
