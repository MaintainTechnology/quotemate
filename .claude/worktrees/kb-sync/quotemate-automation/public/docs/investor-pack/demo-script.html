03

Pre-Answered Investor Questions

Why electrical and plumbing first, not painting or carpentry?
Electrical was the original pilot — NSW has the toughest licence regime in AU which means we de-risk regulatory complexity early. Plumbing was added because Brisbane has a tradie pilot signed up. Both trades have clear "easy 5" job types where auto-quoting is safe. We avoid trades like painting (too many variables) on purpose.
Why portal-first, not voice-first?
Voice agents cost ~$1,500/month per tradie at moderate call volume — STT, TTS, and minutes don't fit SaaS pricing. Portal/SMS works at SaaS margins today. Voice is built and parked, ready to flip on as a premium tier when a tradie's volume justifies it.
What stops a tradie just using ServiceM8 or Tradify?
Those are calendars and invoicing. We're not. The wedge is the AI quote draft — the bit no incumbent has cracked. Once a tradie is hooked on the receptionist, calendar/CRM/invoicing are easy to add. The other way round is much harder.
Is the quote auto-sent or does the tradie review it first?
Pilot mode is auto-send — the customer gets a quote in ~60 seconds, no tradie tap. We chose this for the solo-builder pilot to maximize signal speed and prove the AI's drafting accuracy. Before tradie #2 onboards, the tradie-review-before-send switch flips on — that's the Australian Consumer Law liability shield (accepted quotes are binding contracts). The hook is already in the code; only an env flag changes.
What's the moat?
The pricing book. We don't just sell software — we help each tradie build a structured price list (most owner-operators don't have one). That book gets richer with every quote and is hard for the tradie to take elsewhere. The AI just looks smart; the data is the moat.
How do you make money?
Monthly SaaS subscription per tradie (Twilio + Vapi + Anthropic costs bundled in). Stripe Connect platform fee on every paid deposit going through QuoteMate — switching on once tradie #2 onboards. Voice tier as a premium upsell later.
