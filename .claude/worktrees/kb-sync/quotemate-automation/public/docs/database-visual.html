05The two cookbooks — how a price gets onto a quote

This is the part most people get wrong. The AI doesn't make up prices. It looks them up. There are two cookbooks, the AI reads from both at once, picks the best-matching recipe, then a Price Checker verifies every dollar before the quote can go out. If anything looks fishy, the whole quote dumps to a $99 site visit instead.

The Master Cookbook

shared_assemblies  +  shared_materials

Generic recipes everyone uses. We wrote these by hand. About 43 recipes today.

  • "Install LED downlight"
  • "Replace double power point"
  • "Clear blocked drain"
  • ...

The Tradie's Personal Cookbook

tenant_custom_assemblies  +  tenant_material_catalogue

Specific brands and SKUs this tradie uses. Loaded via CSV or the dashboard form.

  • "MY Clipsal Iconic GPO @ $85"
  • "MY Philips Hue downlight @ $48"
  • "MY custom 'Install pool light'"
  • ...

Smart Sorter

Looks at what the customer asked for and ranks the best-matching recipes from both books, side by side. Tradie's personal recipes win ties.

AI builds the quote
Picks one recipe per tier, applies markup, writes line items. Cannot type a price — can only quote what the cookbooks say.

Price Checker (the safety net)

Reads every single line item and asks 3 questions: Does this price exist in the cookbook? Is it from the right category? Did the AI pick the exact recipe it claimed? If anything fails, the quote is replaced with a $99 site visit.

Pass → Quote sent
Customer sees Good/Better/Best
Fail → $99 site visit
Safer than a wrong price
